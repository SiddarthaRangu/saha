SAHA PRODUCT & CONCEPT DOCUMENT(initial draft)

1\. Product Identity
--------------------

**Name:** Saha

**Meaning:** Support / assistance

**Philosophy:**

Saha exists to reduce the effort spent on job applications so users can invest more time in preparation.

Saha does not promise jobs.

Saha does not manipulate hiring.

Saha supports the process.

**Primary Tagline:**

> We handle applications. You focus on preparation.

* * * * *

2\. Core Problem
----------------

Job seekers today struggle with:

-   Repetitive application work

- Unstructured tools

- Poor resume customization

- Time wasted on form filling

- Less time for real preparation

Most candidates use multiple disconnected tools:

ChatGPT, Google Docs, Excel, Notion, emails.

This leads to:

- Cognitive overload

- Inconsistent quality

- No centralized workflow

* * * * *

3\. Product Vision
------------------

Saha is a **job application assistant platform** that:

-   Analyzes resumes against job descriptions

- Generates tailored application content

- Centralizes job tracking

- Guides preparation based on role and company

Saha focuses on:

Support, structure, and workflow --- not guarantees.

* * * * *

4\. Functional Areas
--------------------

### A. Application Support

Purpose: Reduce effort spent on applying.

Features:

- Resume vs JD analysis

- Skill gap detection

- Resume improvement suggestions

- Cover letter generation

- Application form answers

- Recruiter cold emails

- Referral messages

- LinkedIn outreach messages

* * * * *

### B. Preparation Support

Purpose: Help user prepare once application is done.

Features:

- Role-based concept lists

- Company-based topics

- Interview question suggestions

- Assessment preparation guidance

* * * * *

### C. Workflow Support

Purpose: Centralize job search.

Features:

- Job tracker

- Application status

- Notes & feedback

- History of applications

* * * * *

### D. Cost & Access Support

Purpose: Make Saha usable for students.

Features:

- Guest usage (limited free)

- Registered users

- Usage limits

- Rate limiting

- BYOK (Bring Your Own API Key)

* * * * *

### E. Resume Infrastructure (Later Phase)

Purpose: Reduce resume formatting work.

Features:

- LaTeX-based templates

- Structured resume generation

- Automatic PDF creation

- Resume versioning

* * * * *

5\. BYOK (Bring Your Own Key)
-----------------------------

BYOK allows users to attach their own API keys.

Benefits:

- No subscription

- Unlimited usage

- Zero marginal cost

- Scales infinitely

Supported providers:

- Google Gemini

- OpenAI

BYOK is designed as an **advanced mode**, after core AI features are stable.

* * * * *

6\. Product Principles
----------------------

Saha follows these rules:

-   No fake hiring promises

- No hidden costs

- No forced subscriptions

- No bloated features

- Focus on real workflow

* * * * *

7\. User Types
--------------

### Guest Users:

-   Limited free usage

- No persistent storage

- Encouraged to register

### Registered Users:

-   Full access

- Multiple resumes

- Job tracking

- History saved

* * * * *

8\. Positioning
---------------

Saha is not:

- A job portal

- A resume marketplace

- A recruitment agency

Saha is:

- A workflow system for job seekers

- A support platform for applications

- A preparation assistant
